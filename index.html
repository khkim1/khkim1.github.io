Kun ho Kim

My name is John (Kun ho) Kim and I'm an senior undergraduate student majoring in computer science at Caltech. I work in Professor Pietro Perona's Computational Vision Lab and Professor Joel Burdick's robotics lab. I am currently collaborating with Professor Emma Brunskill at Stanford University. My current research interests are in deep reinforcement learning. In general, I am deeply interested in physics and mathematics.

Previously, I spent a great year as a researcher in the Dept. of Medical Engineering at Caltech with Prof. Hyuck Choo. I developed a biomedical sensor signal processing algorithm using neural networks which enabled real-time readout from a intraocular pressure (IOP) sensing optomechanical implant used for Glaucoma patient diagnosis and monitoring. We built an end-to-end training system which is currently being used to calibrate new generations of IOP sensors.

Before this, I was also a summer research intern at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, closely advised by Dr. Issa Nesnas . I worked on developing discrete-time simulations for rover hybrids exploring small solar system bodies.
