My name is John (Kun ho) Kim and I'm an senior undergraduate student majoring in computer science at Caltech. I work in Professor Pietro Perona's Computational Vision Lab and Professor Joel Burdick's robotics lab. I am currently collaborating with Professor Emma Brunskill at Stanford University. My current research interests are in enabling high-level abstract reasoning for RL agents. In general, I am deeply interested in physics and mathematics.

Previously, I spent a great year as a researcher in the Dept. of Medical Engineering at Caltech with Professor Hyuck Choo. I developed a biomedical sensor signal processing algorithm using neural networks which enabled real-time readout from a intraocular pressure (IOP) sensing optomechanical implant used for Glaucoma patient diagnosis and monitoring. We achieved state-of-the-art IOP readout accuracy and measurement rate surpassing the performance standards of prior sensing technology.

Before this, I was also a summer research intern at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, closely advised by Dr. Issa Nesnas . I worked on developing discrete-time simulations for rover hybrids exploring small solar system bodies.

Publications

High-resolution In Vivo Ocular Pulsation Monitoring Sensor via Intraocular Optical Implant Synchronized to Cardiac Monitoring
Jeong Oen Lee, Kun ho Kim, Ashwin Balakrishna, Juan Du, David Sretavan, Hyuck Choo, coming soon.
Can we monitor the well-being of the cardiac system through our eyes? This question is motivated by numerous practical challenges in current cardiac monitors. We propose using an intraocular implant processed by powerful artificial intelligence signal processing algorithms to extract cardiac indicator variables from the ocular pulse.

Context Embedding Networks
Kun ho Kim, Oisin Mac Aodha, Pietro Perona, in submission.
[PDF]
Can we use the crowd to learn categorical labels for image datasets when annotators use different similarity criterion? We propose the Context Embedding Networks (CEN) model for crowd clustering: learning low-dimensional disentangled embeddings of images in an unlabeled dataset by having non-expert crowd annotators cluster a collection of images.

Real-time In Vivo Intraocular Pressure Monitoring using an Optomechanical Implant and Artificial Neural Networks
Kun ho Kim, Jeong Oen Lee, Juan Du, David Sretavan, Hyuck Choo, IEEE Sensors Journal, vol. 17, no. 22, pp. 7394-7404, 2017.
[PDF]
Demonstrated an end-to-end system to train a fast and robust Artificial Neural Network (DNN) based signal demodulation algorithm for high-temporal real-time intraocular pressure (IOP) readout from optomechanical sensors which are used to monitor Glaucoma progression. Our methods beats all previous IOP sensors in both readout accuracy and sampling rate which greatly enhances the practicality of the sensing approach and enables extraction of high frequency ocular pulses which has been under-explored due to lack of fast readout systems.

Quantifying Performance of Bipedal Standing with Multi-channel EMG
Yanan Sui, Kun ho Kim, Joel Burdick, IROS, 2017.
[PDF]
We demonstrate an end-to-end framework to quantify the bipedal standing performance of patients with motor complete spinal cord injury (SCI) given 12-channel EMG signals and Support Vector Machine (SVM) classifiers.

Sound Analysis of a Cup Drum
Kun ho Kim, European Journal of Physics, vol 33, no. 6, pp. S25-S43, 2012.
[PDF]
Formulated the physics model of cup drum - a musical instrument that can generate different sounds depending on the distance between an inverted cylindrical cavity and a water surface - using wave mechanics and performed experimental validation of the theory.
